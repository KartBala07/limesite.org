Team History

From 15 students in a single classroom to a roster of 100+ with a decade-long streak on the Einstein field. Here's how 1678 got here.

2025

Our robot SubLime swept three regionals during REEFSCAPE — seeding first at and winning Pinnacles, Sacramento, and Contra Costa — and we took the Impact Award at Pinnacles. Our ten-year Einstein streak closed this year, but we built one of our most complex machines yet and finished among the top five in EPA by Championships.

3× Regional WinnerImpact AwardTop 5 EPA
2024

For our 20th anniversary we won the East Bay and Silicon Valley regionals and took the Regional FIRST Impact Award at SVR. At the World Championship we won the Archimedes Division and reached the Einstein field for the 10th straight year, finishing ranked first in the world on Statbotics.

Archimedes Champions10th EinsteinWorld #1 (Statbotics)
2023

With Tangerine Tumbler we won the Hueneme Port, Sacramento, and Silicon Valley regionals. In Houston we ranked first and won the Galileo Division, then advanced to Einstein with a 1–2 record.

Galileo Champions3× Regional Winner
2022

Coming back from the pandemic, we won three regionals — Hueneme Port, Sacramento, and Silicon Valley — and earned the Engineering Inspiration Award for the first time. At Champs we won the Hopper division and reached the Einstein semifinals for the eighth straight year, placing fifth worldwide.

Engineering InspirationHopper Champions5th Worldwide
2021

With in-person events suspended, we competed in the FRC At Home Challenges. We won the Skills Challenge for the Hydrogen Group with some of the fastest times in FRC, were named one of three Game Design winners for our game Storm Surge, and received the Greater Central Valley Chairman's Award.

Skills WinnerGame Design WinnerChairman's Award
2020

We won our first event at the LA North Regional before the season halted abruptly due to COVID-19 — we were waiting to start matches in St. Louis when it was called. We kept meeting on Zoom and started new-member training early, and earned the Chairman's Award at the St. Louis Regional even though the event itself was canceled.

LA North WinnerChairman's Award
2019

We seeded first and won the Sacramento, Central Valley, and Utah regionals, taking the Chairman's and Entrepreneurship awards at Sacramento. We won the Newton Subdivision and reached the Einstein semifinals for the sixth year running, placing third worldwide.

3× Regional WinnerNewton Champions3rd Worldwide
2018

On our 15th anniversary we became the first team ever to reach the Einstein field seven years in a row — winning Central Valley, Sacramento, Aerospace Valley, and the Carver division at Champs. We earned our first Entrepreneurship Award at Worlds and the Chairman's Award at Central Valley, finishing third worldwide.

First-Ever 7× EinsteinCarver Champions3rd Worldwide
2017

We founded the non-profit Davis Robotics Foundation to support local STEM programs and FRC teams. The roster hit 100 students for the first time, we won three regionals and the Newton Subdivision, and finished as World Champion Finalists — extending our Einstein streak to five years.

Davis Robotics FoundationWorld Finalists
2016

A growing team earned a second classroom. We formalized the Davis Youth Robotics League and summer camp programs, drawing over a hundred local students in year one, won three regionals, and took the Hopper Subdivision to reach the Einstein semifinals.

3× Regional WinnerHopper Champions
2015

Our school district became a major sponsor, giving us a large workspace and supporting three robotics classes for the first time. We added five machines, won three regionals, and won the World Championship alongside alliance partners 118 Robonauts, 1671 Bird Brains, and 5012 Gryffingear.

World Champions3× Regional Winner
2011 – 2014

These years built the foundation of a powerhouse. We won our first regional in 2011 and reached the Curie quarterfinals; UC Davis began sponsoring us in 2012 and we launched our Farmers Market booth; new shop machinery in 2013 expanded hands-on training; and in 2014 we won the Newton division, losing only in the final seconds of the last match on Einstein.

First Regional WinUC Davis SponsorNewton Champions
2004 – 2010

Founded in 2004 as EnGen Robotics by Steve Harvey with 15 students, the team spent its early years in a single classroom with one mentor — even buying a 20-foot shipping container as a workspace in 2008. From 2005 to 2010 we attended only the Sacramento Regional each year, often serving as alliance captain but never advancing past the quarterfinals.
