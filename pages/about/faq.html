FRC assigns every team a number based on the order it joined the competition — we were the 1,678th team to sign up. FIRST uses that number to identify us at events and on leaderboards.
FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) is a non-profit founded by Dean Kamen to get students of all ages excited about robotics and technology. It runs several programs; FRC — the FIRST Robotics Competition — is the flagship high-school program we compete in. You can read more at firstinspires.org.
The team exists to get young people excited about science, technology, engineering, and math. Competing gives us hands-on experience in everything from mechanical engineering to programming and pushes us to solve problems creatively under real constraints of time and materials. FRC students are also eligible for millions of dollars in college scholarships. Mostly, though — it's fun.
Membership is by application and interview, open to local students in grades 9–12. Members are expected to attend at least 80% of meetings — Wednesdays and Thursdays 6:30–9 p.m. in Room M-1 at Davis Senior High School, plus weekends 9 a.m.–5 p.m. from January through April. Applications open in spring. See our Recruitment page or email admin@citruscircuits.org for the coming season.
The Advanced Robotics Engineering classes are "8th period" courses at Davis Senior High School. They're a way for students to earn high-school credit for the after-school and weekend time they put into the competition.

No — FIRST games don't involve attacking other robots. It's closer to robotic soccer: two alliances of three robots each compete on an indoor field, and there are penalties for deliberately damaging another robot. Alliances even shift during an event, so today's opponent may be tomorrow's partner. The goal is creative problem-solving and teamwork, not building the deadliest machine.
They're core FIRST values. Gracious professionalism means behaving maturely and respectfully toward other teams and officials — and helping them out, whether by lending parts or hands, so everyone can compete. Coopertition is the idea of competing cooperatively: teams work alongside others in alliances even while competing. Both are woven so deeply into FIRST culture that there are formal awards for teams that exemplify them.
