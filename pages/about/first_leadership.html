FIRST

FIRST® (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) is a global robotics community founded in 1989 by inventor Dean Kamen. FIRST designs accessible, innovative programs that build self-confidence, knowledge, and life skills in young people while motivating them to pursue education and career opportunities in science, technology, engineering, and math.

"I can't think of a more important thing to do than try to change the culture, to get kids as excited about academic pursuits as they are about sports."— Dean Kamen, FIRST Founder

FIRST programs — including the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC), FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC), and FIRST LEGO League (FLL) — culminate each year at the FIRST Championship in Houston, Texas, where hundreds of thousands of students from around the world compete and celebrate together.

At the heart of every FIRST program are the core values of Gracious Professionalism® and Coopertition®: the idea that teams can be fierce competitors on the field while still helping, respecting, and learning from one another off of it.

Mentoring the Next Generation

Many Citrus Circuits alumni return as mentors, while current members mentor Davis Youth Robotics (DYR) and rookie FRC teams. The team also helps start new FRC and FTC programs across the region.

Event & Committee Roles

Team members and mentors serve as FIRST volunteers, judges, and event organizers at regionals, districts, and Championship — including running our own off-season event, Capital City Classic.
