INFINITE RECHARGE • 2021 Season

Clemen-twins

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the 2021 FRC season was held as an at-home challenge. Citrus Circuits fielded two competition robots — Clemen-Tiny and Clemen-Teeny — each optimized to perform different parts of the INFINITE RECHARGE challenge.

CLEMEN-TWINS
Overview

Two Robots, One Season

The 2021 INFINITE RECHARGE AT HOME challenge required teams to complete specific tasks independently rather than compete head-to-head. To maximize their score across different challenges, Citrus Circuits designed and built two distinct robots: Clemen-Tiny and Clemen-Teeny.

Each robot was purpose-built with unique drivetrains and mechanisms, allowing the team to specialize for different challenge categories. Both robots contributed to the team's Skills Challenge performance.

Clemen-Tiny

CLEMEN-TINY
Competition Robot

Clemen-Tiny

Clemen-Tiny was built around a 12-Falcon swerve drivetrain, achieving a maximum speed of 17 ft/sec. Its compact 17.5″×17.5″ frame made it highly maneuverable and well-suited for the precision tasks in the at-home challenge.

The swerve modules provided omnidirectional movement, enabling the robot to approach targets from any angle without reorienting the chassis — a critical advantage in a timed, single-robot format.

Clemen-Teeny

CLEMEN-TEENY
Competition Robot

Clemen-Teeny

Clemen-Teeny was built using an FTC Treadrunner-style drivetrain with 8 Falcons, pushing its top speed to 21 ft/sec — the fastest of the two robots. Its design emphasized raw speed and reliability for high-throughput challenge runs.

The track-style drivetrain gave Clemen-Teeny exceptional straight-line stability and the power needed to handle rapid acceleration during timed runs.

Scoring

Both robots were built to score Power Cells (foam balls) into the Power Port goals at various distances. Clemen-Tiny focused on precision shooting with its swerve drivetrain enabling dynamic positioning. Clemen-Teeny leveraged speed to maximize Power Cell intake and cycling rates in allotted challenge windows.

The at-home challenge format rewarded accuracy and cycle speed, making the two-robot strategy particularly effective for capturing points across multiple challenge categories.

Drivetrain

Clemen-Tiny used a 12-Falcon swerve drivetrain with a 17.5″×17.5″ frame, topping out at 17 ft/sec. The swerve modules allowed full omnidirectional movement without changing robot heading.

Clemen-Teeny featured an FTC Treadrunner-inspired tank drivetrain powered by 8 Falcons, reaching 21 ft/sec. The tracked design provided high traction and consistent performance across challenge surfaces.

Operating Interface

Both robots were operated using Xbox controllers. For the at-home challenge, drivers were positioned trackside for direct line-of-sight operation during timed runs. Clemen-Tiny's swerve controls were mapped to give operators independent heading and translation control, while Clemen-Teeny's simpler drivetrain used a traditional tank-style layout for maximum speed.

Electrical

Clemen-Tiny's 12-Falcon motor count required careful power budgeting and bus management. The team used a modular wiring layout to keep harness weight down within the compact 17.5″×17.5″ frame. Clemen-Teeny's 8-Falcon drivetrain was wired for peak current draw during sprint runs, with fuse and breaker sizing chosen to support repeated high-amperage acceleration without nuisance trips.
