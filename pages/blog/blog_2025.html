With only one week before our first competition, subteams are working hard to finish our competition robot before Pinnacles Regional.

Hardware Design

Design is implementing multiple changes to Comp, including a new end effector design enhanced by MAXComposite, switching indexer and intake motors, and considering better intake options. Additionally, they have been cutting spare parts for Comp and working with Electrical to wire the robot.

Hardware Fabrication

Hardware Fabrication has been working on finishing parts for both Comp and Beta upgrades. They also started working on the spare parts for Comp with the goal of finishing everything this weekend.

Hardware Electrical

Hardware Electrical has been assisting Design with multiple upgrades, swaps, and changes being made to Comp. They are rewiring the swapped mechanisms, improving the reliability of our competition robot, and preparing to upgrade our Beta robot.

Software Scouting

Front-End ran a full system field test for our Match Collection app and is implementing new features including bigger buttons and different colors. Back-End also ran a full system field test, fixed the last few bugs found during testing, and started extensive stress testing ahead of Pinnacles Regional.

Software Robot

Software Robot successfully implemented coral detection on our competition robot. They are updating sequencing for the newest iteration of Comp and working on both single and multi-piece autos.

Business & Media

BAM recently released an Instagram announcement video for our newest sponsor: The Sacramento Kings! In the first three days, the post gained 20,000 views and more than 800 shares. They continue to take media to document the progress of our robot for future videos.

Strategy

Strategy has been pre-scouting teams attending Pinnacles Regional and analyzing Week 0 events to better understand expected performance and craft new match strategies.

Impact Award

Impact Award has been preparing for the Pinnacles interview. The presentation team practiced in front of a parent panel acting as mock judges. The team designed a prop featuring team information, photos, graphs, moving LED lights, and a glowing lime in the center — nearing completion with help from shop mentors.

With just under two weeks before our first competition, our team is working hard to finish our Comp robot!

Hardware Design

Hardware Design is making all final revisions to Comp. They plan to upgrade Beta with Comp's features — altering the indexer to stabilize it when intaking coral, modifying the rollers, and continuing to test and reiterate the End-Effector design. Once Comp is brought up by Software, they plan to test and reiterate or pivot to a different design.

Hardware Fabrication

Hardware Fabrication has been hard at work fabricating parts for our Comp and mega defense robot. They have also started creating parts for our updated Beta robot.

Hardware Electrical

Hardware Electrical finished wiring our Comp robot. They have been creating a signal loop for our motor system, fixing LED strips onto the robot to signal the driver, and wiring the new Limelight 4.0 cameras for our vision system. They are preparing to re-wire portions of Beta as Design plans to upgrade certain features.

Software Scouting

Front-End is fixing last bugs in Match Collection and adding user-friendly features. They are continuing to finish auto paths on Viewer and testing both the Stand Strategist and Subjective Scout apps in preparation for a full system field test. Back-End finished most projects including machine learning for match predictions, a new elimination bracket prediction system, and pickability calculations. They began a series of full-system field tests for Pinnacles.

Software Robot

Software Robot just brought up the Comp robot, SubLime! They are working on porting all code, tuning auto align setpoints on Beta, building an improved autonomous architecture, mechanism visualization for Comp, and updating their superstructure planning.

Business & Media

BAM has been continuing work on the Robot Reveal and Impact videos, handling merchandise sales, budgeting, designing the sponsor panel and pit display, and transferring our website to another hosting provider. They are also finishing the February newsletter.

Strategy

Strategy has been analyzing 2023 and 2024 data to predict team abilities at Pinnacles Regional and watching Week 0 events to gauge robot capabilities.

Kickoff

On January 4, our team participated in the kickoff for the worldwide reveal of the 2025 game Reefscape. In the morning, we hosted 15 other teams at Brunelle Hall to watch the reveal livestream. Throughout the weekend, we split into small groups to read and analyze the game manual and determine what our robot should do.

About REEFSCAPE

In Reefscape (presented by Haas), two alliances of three teams compete to earn points by harvesting Algae (16″ rubber playground balls) and seeding Coral (12″ by 4″ PVC pipe) on their Reef. Alliances also earn points by parking underneath or climbing onto Deep (3-1/8 in. off the ground) or Shallow (2 ft. 6-1/8 in.) cages to ascend to the ocean's surface.

Hardware Design

Hardware Design completed the architecture for Beta, dividing into smaller prototyping groups to focus on testing key mechanisms including the Algae and Coral intakes and both high and low pivot climbs.

Hardware Fabrication

Fabrication members spent the week machining wood parts for prototyping and starting parts for Alpha, the first robot in our design process that consists of a drivebase with preliminary mechanism designs.

Hardware Electrical

Electrical subteam members have been assisting in prototyping our 2025 robot, running motors off prototyping rigs, and assisting with the overall prototyping and documentation process.

Software Scouting

Front-End is currently updating our Match Collection app to reflect the 2025 game, Reefscape, and improving our Pit Collection and Stand Strategist apps. Back-End has been determining data points for this year and creating new calculations for Reefscape's unique scoring mechanics.

Software Robot

Software Robot has been brainstorming auto-align options, how to approach odometry and vision implementations, and updating all Alpha contenders as hardware determines which robot becomes Alpha.

Business & Media

BAM members have been assisting in prototyping our 2025 robot, running motors off prototyping rigs, and assisting with the overall prototyping and documentation process.

Strategy

The Strategy subteam has been analyzing the nuances and potential exploits within the Reefscape game rules, refining match flow strategies, and beginning to create simulations to optimize robot performance.

Impact Award
