Season Recap

2016

Hopper Division Champions4th Consecutive Einstein as #1 AllianceEinstein Semi-Finalists3× Regional WinnerWoodie Flowers Finalist Award

Houston World Championship Results

The end of the 2016 season brought tremendous glory for Team 1678 Citrus Circuits! This is the fourth consecutive year we made it to the Einstein Field as the first seeded alliance captain or first pick, a feat that we are the first to accomplish.

Our team competed in the Hopper sub-division, ranking third after qualifications with the highest autonomous score in the entire division. We unleashed our two ball autonomous routine for the first time in competition. During alliance selections, we were picked by Team 148 Robowranglers, from Greenville, Texas, along with 364 Team Fusion from Gulfport, Mississippi, and Team 2990 Hotwire from Aumsville, Oregon. Together, we won the Hopper Division, defeating Team 971 Spartan Robotics in an exhilarating series of final matches.

In Hopper Quarterfinals 1 Match 2, we achieved the highest score without penalties in the world, winning with an astonishing total of 270 that included the highest goal count of any match this season with 24 high goals.

Our success continued onward into the Einstein Field. However, after three grueling matches, we ultimately lost to the current 2016 World Champion alliance. We would like to give a huge thank you to Team 971 Spartan Robotics and their amazing mentor Austin Schuh for the exceptional act of gracious professionalism in helping us troubleshoot problems with our robot during the semi-final matches on Einstein Field.

Silicon Valley Regional Results

Our team won its third blue banner at the Silicon Valley Regional in San Jose, where we faced intense competition from some of the best teams from California, along with teams from Hawaii, Turkey, and China. After highly competitive qualification matches, we seeded first among the 66 teams at the competition, allowing us to choose alliance partners Team 254 Cheesy Poofs and Team 1662 Raptor Force Engineering from Lodi. Together, we were undefeated in the elimination round and had the highest score of the competition with 212 points.

Additionally, we won the Excellence in Engineering Award, which recognizes the engineering and programming of the robot's extendable shooting arm, providing us with an unblockable shot.

Sacramento Regional Results

Our team earned its second blue banner of the season at the Sacramento Regional in the UC Davis Pavilion on March 23 to 26, with twelve wins, one loss, and one tie. We were seeded fourth at the end of qualifications and were the first pick by Team 971 Spartan Robotics from Mountain View. Rounding out the alliance was Team 5274 Wolverines from Sacramento. Together, the alliance went undefeated in elimination rounds and dominated in the final rounds, setting a high score of 205 on the way to becoming 2016 Sacramento Regional Winners.

Along with the blue banner, we also won the Excellence in Engineering Award. Michael Corsetto, our head technical mentor and drive coach, was recognized with the prestigious Woodie Flowers Finalist Award, presented to a mentor that best demonstrates leading, inspiring, teaching, and empowering their team using excellent communication skills.

Central Valley Regional Results

At the Central Valley Regional Competition in Madera, our team competed with 48 other teams from California. We claimed first place in the start of the competition and firmly held onto that ranking, seeding first after qualification matches. This allowed us to choose our alliance partners: Team 254 Cheesy Poofs from San Jose, and Team 3970 Duncan Dynamics from Fresno. Together we won the event and earned our first blue banner of the season.
